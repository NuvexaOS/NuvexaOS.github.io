Why It Feels So Fast

Traditional monolithic kernels juggle drivers, memory, scheduling and more inside one space—great for 1991, not so much today. NuvexaOS flips that by running drivers and system services in isolated micro-servers. If your Wi-Fi driver seg-faults, your desktop is still alive.

  • Pre-emptive zero-copy channels slash context-switch overhead.
  • MsgQ batching coalesces interrupts—lower latency on AMD & Intel CPUs.
  • zRAM 2 transparently compresses idle pages, saving up to 40 % RAM.

Security That Doesn’t Nag

Forget root passwords on every sudo. The Nux Policy Engine evaluates app manifests once at launch, then silently enforces them via eBPF filters. Less “Allow/Deny?” pop-ups, more coding—or gaming.

  • Per-app VPN routing
  • Automatic key-rotation for SSH/PGP
  • Kernel-level anti-cheat bypass for online games (so BattleEye’s happy)

Design Language: HoloGlass™

Windows float like glass shards lit by distant nebulae. Yet everything stays crisp on SDR and HDR monitors thanks to dynamic tone-mapping. Oh, and yes—animations are 120 Hz-ready.
